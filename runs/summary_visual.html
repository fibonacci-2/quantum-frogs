⏱️

Quantized time rewards decisive play

Cars only move when you do. Every agent that learned to win did so by rushing straight up in ~7 steps. Hesitation is a self-inflicted penalty.

🤷

Two players without communication is harder than six cars alone

IDQN at 1 car (43%) loses to DQN at 6 cars (59%). A second frog multiplies difficulty unless both players coordinate.

🤝

Cooperation = discovering the same simple plan

MAPPO didn't learn complex tactics. It learned that both frogs should rush simultaneously — eliminating the non-stationarity that prevented independent agents from converging.

🎯

Optimal difficulty window: 2–4 cars co-op

Below 2 cars even uncoordinated players can get lucky. Above 5 cars even perfect coordination struggles. The 2–4 range is where coordination skill actually determines outcome.
